# Pitch Deck Blueprint — Trickle_Down

Synthesized from research on Sequoia/YC deck structure, alt-data pitching,
deck design tells, and narrative craft. Sources at bottom.

## What separates top-decile decks (the rules I'm building to)
1. **A deck is an argument, not a presentation.** Every slide makes ONE claim
   and shows the evidence for it. The slides in sequence are a logical case:
   tension → thesis → what we built → proof → who pays → why defensible → ask.
2. **Data assets don't sell data — they sell an earlier decision.** Investors/
   buyers weight one question above all: *does this give a signal earlier than
   the sources they already have?* Our whole hook is one sentence: a read on
   the apparel cycle **10–11 months** before the government prints it.
3. **Proof-of-signal is the most-scrutinized slide and the one thing that
   can't be modeled or reverse-engineered.** Our pre-registered, walk-forward
   validation IS that proof. Lead with it; don't bury it.
4. **Narrative tension is 40% more memorable.** Set the 127-year-old
   unmeasured claim as tension in the first three slides; resolve it with the
   measurement. One killer number, stated once, big.
5. **Honesty as differentiator.** A slide that says "here is what does NOT
   work" — three trading strategies, killed under rules set in advance — reads
   as rigor, not weakness. Almost no data pitch does this; it builds the trust
   the rest of the deck spends.
6. **Match his baseline (Covos) and beat it.** His deck is tight, quant-heavy,
   hard numbers, bottoms-up market (not top-down TAM), clean competition grid.
   Keep all of that discipline; exceed the narrative arc and the craft.

## Banned clichés (design tells to avoid)
- Everything centered "because centering is safe" → use deliberate LEFT-aligned
  asymmetry, big number one side / text the other.
- Warm cream #F4F1EA + serif + terracotta (the canonical AI-deck look) → cooler
  flax ground, indigo ink, earthy multi-material accents instead of terracotta.
- Near-black + a lone acid-green/vermilion pop → accents are the *material*
  palette (ochre, teal, rust), never one neon.
- Inter/Space Grotesk "safe" faces; system-font drift; gray-on-brown text.
- Bullet spam; mismatched font sizes; generic stock imagery; template sameness;
  emoji section markers; rounded-lg cards with an accent rail.
- TAM cones with no bottoms-up basis; hockey-stick charts with no data behind.

## Design system (chosen for this subject)
- **Ground** `#e9e6dc` cool flax/calico (green-grey bias — a mill's raw canvas,
  deliberately NOT warm cream). Ink `#1a1d23` indigo-black. Hairlines `#c7c2b3`
  like a textile spec sheet. Faint warp/weft grid on dividers only.
- **Signal accent** raw-denim indigo `#284079` — used ONLY on the killer
  numbers and the emphasized data mark. Denim is literally a material in the
  taxonomy; it reads craft + rigor, not corporate.
- **Material threads** (data/loom only) — the validated palette: wool #B5762E,
  viscose #0E9080, leather #96430E, cotton #8A7A14, nylon #6C5BB5, silk #2E8FC4.
- **Type** — Display: high-contrast serif (Bodoni 72 / Charter / Georgia) for
  statements; Data/labels: ui-monospace with tabular-nums; uppercase mono
  eyebrows with letter-spacing. Confidence comes from BIG type + whitespace +
  one idea per slide, not decoration.
- **Motif** — the cascade "loom": material threads flowing runway → rack → mill,
  bending at the fitted lag. Hero (faint, full-bleed) and the cascade-proof
  slide (foreground, labeled). Fabric textures (twill/knit/grain) fill the
  loom threads, tying to the subject.
- **Format** — 1280×720 (16:9) landscape slides, spec-sheet footer with slide
  number + running title, page-break per slide → PDF.

## Slide outline (13, argument arc)
1. Cover — "Trickle Down — the fashion cascade, measured." faint loom, byline.
2. Tension — the 127-yr-old claim nobody measured (Veblen 1899).
3. Thesis / killer number — a leading read on apparel demand, 10–11 months out.
4. What we built — the three-tier asset (looks → archived labels → prices).
5. The novel method — the Internet Archive as a point-in-time assortment record.
6. Proof I — the cascade is real: the loom + fitted lags (43 links, wool +5mo).
7. Proof II — the leading indicator: turning-point nowcast vs baselines.
8. The honest part — three ways it does NOT trade; why that earns trust.
9. Who pays — trend vendors / textile / alt-data; Heuritech €12–35k/yr anchor.
10. Why defensible — the corpus can't be re-bought; deepens every season.
11. What you'd get — the productized feed (indicator, lag tables, alerts).
12. Why now — cheap vision tagging + deep archive + demand-planning pressure.
13. Close / ask — design partners / pilot; the method is public (repo, paper).
