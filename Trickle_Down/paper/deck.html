The question

Everyone knows fashion trickles down.
Nobody had measured how long it takes.

Veblen sketched it in 1899. Simmel formalized it in 1904. The trade press repeats it every season: a look leaves the runway, lands in Zara a few seasons later, and eventually moves order books at the mills that spin the fiber.

In 127 years, no one published how many months, at what strength, for which materials.

Why it's new

The retailers never kept the history.
The Internet Archive did.

Fast-fashion sites overwrite. There is no record of what Zara stocked in 2018 — except that the Internet Archive crawled and froze those product pages, fabric labels and all, with a timestamp on every one.

We read 11,229 composition labels off archived pages to rebuild 267 months of what the high street actually sold. As far as the literature shows, nobody has used the Archive this way — as a point-in-time record of commercial assortment.

The prior art fits lags of at most two months, between cities or brands. None fits the multi-quarter jump across tiers. This does.

Proof · the leading indicator

It calls apparel turning points before the data does.

Against twelve peaks and troughs in U.S. clothing-store sales growth, the cascade signal leads by 10–11 months at 0.46 precision and 0.42 recall — catching 5 of 12.

An AR(3) baseline manages 0.14 and 0.08. Persistence catches nothing.

Drop the four COVID reversals — the easy hits — and it still runs 0.30 / 0.38 on eight turning points.

What does NOT work

We tried to trade it three ways. All three failed — under rules we set in advance.

✕
Continuous nowcast

All nine pre-registered parameter sets lost money in walk-forward validation. Best information ratio −0.39.

✕
Adoption factor

The archive supports depth on only two retailers. A two-name cross-section is a pair trade, not a factor.

✕
Earnings event study

Headline +3.1% per event — but a pre-event placebo window shows a larger move. The announcement isn't the cause.

We publish the failures at the same size as the wins. That is the point: every number here survived a rule written before we looked. The forecast is what's left standing.
